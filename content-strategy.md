## Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice (Constant)

The brand voice is bold, high-energy, adventurous, active, and down-to-earth. It sounds like an energetic high school senior sharing his outdoor exploits directly with his peers and community. It is direct, action-oriented, and authentic—never formal, stuffy, or overly academic.

**Voice attributes:**
- **Adventurous** — Focused on hands-on experiences. *Example: "If it's in the creek or the trailer, I'm catching it."*
- **Active** — Constantly moving and pushing limits. *Example: "From 20-mile bike rides to catching snakes in the creek, I'm always on the move."*
- **Direct & Honest** — Straightforward, punchy phrasing. *Example: "I don't just ride—I go the distance."*
- **Enthusiastic** — Authentic excitement for parks and wildlife. *Example: "My absolute favorite park of all time!"*

### Tone Shifts by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Homepage hero | Bold & High-Octane | "Chasing Adventure & Catching Animals" |
| Bio / School | Proud & Motivated | "Getting Student of the Month was a major goal of mine, and it reflects how I approach everything: with 100% energy." |

| Trail Life / Outdoors | Active & Team-focused | "Adventure Backpacking: It might look the same everywhere, but the trek is what matters!" |
| Travel / Explorations | Curious & Rugged | "Creek Scouting: Finding hidden waters in the desert." |

## Content Types & Standards

### Content Type 1 — Catch Log Cards

**Purpose:** Document local wildlife encounters to highlight outdoor handling skills, quick reflexes, and neighborhood rescues.

**Publishing cadence:** As new catches occur.

**Structure:**
- **Title:** The common name of the animal or a fun nickname (e.g. "Tony the Turtle").
- **Visual:** High-impact photo of the capture.
- **Body:** 1-2 sentence description of where it was caught and a detail about its behavior (e.g. softshell, sharp teeth, fast movement).

**Tone:** Rugged, fun, and casual.

---

### Content Type 2 — Travel & Exploration Cards

**Purpose:** Log major camping trips, state/national park treks, and outdoor destinations.

**Publishing cadence:** Post-trip.

**Structure:**
- **Category Badge:** Location or type of trip (e.g., "Arches / Zion", "Deep Caving").
- **Title:** The name of the trek or destination (e.g., "High Adventure Trek").
- **Caption:** An inspiring or descriptive line about the landscape or experience.

**Tone:** Curious, active, and scenery-focused.

---

### Content Type 3 — Hobbies & Academic Milestones

**Purpose:** Display personal accomplishments, sports attendance, and high school highlights.

**Publishing cadence:** When major goals are reached or seasonal events occur.

**Structure:**
- **Visual:** A candid photograph of the milestone or event (e.g., Student of the Month, Aggies game).
- **Description:** Direct connection to personal drive or team spirit.

**Tone:** Proud, enthusiastic, and authentic.

## Microcopy Standards

**Button labels:** Use direct, high-energy actions.
- "The Catch List" — not "View Animals"
- "Fast & Active" — not "Hobbies"
- "Visit TPWD →" — not "Click Here"

**Form labels:** None currently on the page.

**Placeholder text:** None currently on the page.

**Error messages:** None currently.

**Empty states:** (If cards are missing)
- "Still out tracking..." + "Check back after the next trip!"

**Navigation labels:** Single-word, direct links. Max 2 words (e.g., "Bio", "Catches", "Active", "Travels").

## Words We Use / Words We Don't

| Use | Don't Use | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Chasing | Trying to find | "Chasing" implies high energy and active pursuing. |
| Catching / Relocation | Fishing / Hunting | Fits the focus on catching creek animals and releasing/moving them safely. |
| Tackled | Did / Completed | Shows determination and physical effort. |
| Go the distance | Ride a lot | Emphasizes endurance and high standards. |
| Ultimate Goal | Dream | Sounds more action-oriented and structured. |
